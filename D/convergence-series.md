**Definition:** Given a sequence $(a_n)$, the **infinite series** $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n$ is said to **converge** to a sum $S \in \mathbb{R}$ if the sequence of [partial sums](../D/partial-sums) $(s_n)$ converges to $S$:

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n = S \iff \lim_{n \to \infty} s_n = S$$

If $(s_n)$ diverges, the series **diverges**.

**Remark:** It is important to distinguish between the sequence $(a_n)$ and the series $\sum a_n$. The sequence is just a list of terms; the series is the limit of their cumulative sums.
