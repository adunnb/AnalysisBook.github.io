**Definition:** The **natural numbers** are the set:

$$\mathbb{N} = \{1, 2, 3, 4, \ldots\}$$

The **integers** are the set:

$$\mathbb{Z} = \{\ldots, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, \ldots\}$$

The **rational numbers** are the set of all fractions of integers:

$$\mathbb{Q} = \left\{\frac{p}{q} : p, q \in \mathbb{Z},\ q \neq 0\right\}$$

These sets satisfy $\mathbb{N} \subset \mathbb{Z} \subset \mathbb{Q} \subset \mathbb{R}$.

**Remark:** Whether $0 \in \mathbb{N}$ is a matter of convention. In this book we follow the convention that $\mathbb{N}$ starts at $1$. The natural numbers are closed under addition and multiplication but not subtraction or division. The integers are closed under addition, subtraction, and multiplication but not division. The rationals are closed under all four arithmetic operations except division by zero.
