**Definition:** A **power series** centered at $a \in \mathbb{R}$ is a series of the form:

$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c_n (x - a)^n = c_0 + c_1(x-a) + c_2(x-a)^2 + \cdots$$

where $(c_n)$ is a sequence of real coefficients and $x \in \mathbb{R}$.

**Remark:** A power series defines a function of $x$ on the set of all $x$ for which the series converges. This set is always an interval centered at $a$, determined by the [radius of convergence](../D/radius-convergence). At $x = a$ the series always converges to $c_0$.
