**Definition:** Let $A \subseteq \mathbb{R}$ be a non-empty set. A number $b \in \mathbb{R}$ is called a **lower bound** of $A$ if:

$$b \leq a \quad \text{for all } a \in A$$

The set $A$ is **bounded below** if a lower bound exists. A number $i \in \mathbb{R}$ is called the **infimum** (or greatest lower bound) of $A$, written $i = \inf A$, if:

1. $i$ is a lower bound of $A$, and
2. if $b$ is any lower bound of $A$, then $i \geq b$

**Remark:** The infimum is the mirror image of the [supremum](../D/supremum). A set is **bounded** if it is both bounded above and bounded below.
