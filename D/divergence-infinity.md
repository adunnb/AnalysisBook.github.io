title: "Divergence to Infinity"
chapter: "Sequences and Series"
---

**Definition:** A sequence $(a_n)$ of real numbers **diverges to positive infinity**, written $a_n \to +\infty$, if for every $M > 0$ there exists $N \in \mathbb{N}$ such that:

$$n > N \implies a_n > M$$

Similarly, $(a_n)$ **diverges to negative infinity**, written $a_n \to -\infty$, if for every $M > 0$ there exists $N \in \mathbb{N}$ such that:

$$n > N \implies a_n < -M$$

**Remark:** Divergence to infinity is a specific type of divergence -- the sequence grows without bound in a controlled direction. This is distinct from sequences that diverge by oscillation, like $a_n = (-1)^n$, which neither converges nor diverges to infinity.
